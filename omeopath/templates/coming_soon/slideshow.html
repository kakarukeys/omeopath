Our Frustration ~1

  • GERD is difficult to treat.

GERD is largely treated symptomatically. The goal of the current standard treatment protocol is to suppress the acid in the stomach thereby reducing the symptoms. It is not addressing the root cause or mechanism of how acid reflux happens, because the root cause is still poorly understood. Many patients are put on indefinitely long period of acid-suppressing medication to prevent recurrence of their symptoms. Some patients still experience their symptoms despite taking a high dosage of the medication.

Our Frustration ~2

  • Doctors seldom personalize a treatment.

Every patient has his own unique profile due to differences in symptom manifestation, reaction to medicine, general physique, etc. A treatment plan that works best on one patient sometimes does not work optimally on another. However after a consultation, a doctor usually pushes the same standard treatment to his patient and waits until the next consultation to find out the response and adjust accordingly. As a result, the patient did not get the best treatment tailored to him.

GERD

Gastroesophageal reflux disease is a condition that happens when the esophagus is repeatedly exposed to the acidic content from the stomach for a prolonged period of time. It can cause heartburn (a typical symptom) or coughing, throat irritation, etc (atypical symptoms). A chronic GERD condition can result in a low quality of life and may progress into even worse conditions. The exact root cause of GERD is unknown. Usually GERD can be controlled through lifestyle modification and medication. Various surgical options are available for patients who have no luck with medication. A cure for GERD is yet to be found. However through information exchange, we can help each other better understand, manage the disease and achieve healing. This is usually the first step towards discovering a cure.
